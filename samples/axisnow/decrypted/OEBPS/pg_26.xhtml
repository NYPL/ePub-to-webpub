“The soldiers were here,” I said, when Mama and Papa returned.
“They are looking for hidden Jews.”
“Brave Anett!” Papa hugged me. “Our friends must leave tonight
even though it is cloudy. How can we get them safely to the
harbor?”
I thought about being afraid of the dark cellar, and how the
whispering voices guided me down the stairs.
“Papa, what if people stood in their doorways and used their
voices to guide our friends to the boat?” I suggested.
Papa stood quietly, considering my idea.
“That might work,” he said. “Help me to arrange it.”
